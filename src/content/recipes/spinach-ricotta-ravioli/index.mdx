1. Tip the flour into a mound on your worksurface, making a well in the middle. Crack all 3 eggs into the well and add the extra yolk.
2. Using a fork, whisk the eggs like you're making scrabled eggs. As you whisk, progressively bring in flour from the edges and incorporate into the eggs.
3. If its sticky, add a little more flour, if its too dry or hard to knead, add a little water teaspoon by teaspoon until it's got a doughy consistency.
4. Once its smooth, put the dough in a bowl with plastic wrap and leave it for 30 mins or so.
5. For the filling, wilt down a bag of spinach, chop it finely, drain the water out of it and combine with ricotta. Store the filling for later to add to the ravioli. For an extra kick add some minced garlic.
6. Roll the pasta dough out with a pasta machine into flat sheets. With the flat sheets laid out on some light flouring, pipe out the filling into equally spaced sections. Once spaced equally, lay a sheet on top of the base sheet with the filling to create the raviolio. Do your best to ensure there's no air inside the ravioli and when cutting, ensure there's no gaps between the top and bottom sheet.
7. Roast a head of garlic. Chop the top of the top off, brush with oil, salt and pepper the head. Wrap in foil and stick it in the oven. 200 degrees for roughly 40 mins.
8. For the sauce, soften a shallot in a pan with the avocado oil. When softened, add a couple cloves of minced garlic, once fragrant, add the white wine and reduce it. When the wine is reduced, add the chicken stock and let it all simmer for a couple of minutes.
9. As the sauce is simmering, get a pot on the stove on high heat and get water to a boil. Add the ravioli to the water and reduce the water to a gentle simmer. Cook the ravioli until it floats to the top and is tender, this will probably take around 3 mins. Once done, drain the ravioli.
10. Once the stock has cooked down a bit, turn the heat down, add the cream and the parmesan, then salt and pepper to taste.
11. Add the ravioli to the sauce and stir them in gently to ensure they do not break. Serve in a bowl and sprinkle some parmesan on top.
