1. Heat a high walled stainless steel pan, add the oil and onions. When the onions are about half softened, add the garlic.
2. When fragrant and softened fully, add the arborio rice to the pan and coat well in the oily oniony garlicy goodness.
3. Let the rice good for a minute or so and when the rice starts to turn translcent, add the wine and reduce to a simmer. Let this reduce (without stirring) until most of the liquid has been absorbed.
4. Add stock gradually, 1 cup (~250ml) at a time, allow for the stock to be absorbed almost completely before adding the next cup.
5. Continue this until the rice is cooked, probably 30-40 minutes but you can test it to make sure the rice is cooked to check for doneness.
6. After the last cup of stock, add the parmesan to the skillet and season w sea salt and freshly ground black pepper.
7. For the scallops: Heat some oil in a nonstick skillet. Pat the scallops dry (as dry as possible), sprinkle with salt and add to the pan. They should sizzle, if not the pan needs to be hotter. Shake gently to prevent sticking.
8. After 2-3 minutes, flip each scallop over. They should be pretty golden brown and feel opaque inside. Transfer to a paper towel lined plate to absorb excess oil and serve immediately.
